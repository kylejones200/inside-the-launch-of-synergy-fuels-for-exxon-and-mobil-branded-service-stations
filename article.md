### Inside the Launch of Synergy Fuels for Exxon- and Mobil-branded Service Stations
In 2016, ExxonMobil rolled out Synergy fuels across its retail network.
On paper, it was a product launch: seven ingredients blended to clean
engines, reduce emissions, and improve mileage. In practice, it was one
of the largest coordinated campaigns the company had taken on in years.
I had the opportunity to lead the public relations and external
communications strategy for the launch, working with teams in multiple
countries and across business functions.

### Building More Than a Fuel
The fuel itself carried a technical story. The same research team that
supplied Formula 1 racing fuels developed the Synergy formulation. They
created an additive package where each ingredient had a specific
purpose --- detergents to prevent adhesion inside the engine, cleaners
for intake valves, and compounds designed to optimize mileage.

My role was to make sure that science translated into customer language.
That meant working side by side with engineers, marketers, and agency
partners to frame the story in a way that would resonate with drivers,
station owners, and media outlets.

### Scaling Across Markets
We didn't stop at the U.S. The Synergy launch extended to 14 countries.
Each market had unique challenges: regulatory requirements, competitive
positioning, and consumer expectations. I coordinated with local teams
to adapt messages while keeping the global campaign consistent. In some
cases, this meant tailoring stories for highly competitive markets like
Europe, while in others it meant introducing the Synergy brand to
customers who had never seen ExxonMobil fuel products before.

### Partnerships That Extended the Brand
We tied the launch to partnerships that signaled innovation.
Collaborations with Apple, Samsung, IBM, General Motors, Toyota, Ford,
and Red Bull Racing allowed us to place Synergy alongside leading
technology and automotive brands. Those partnerships amplified the
story: ExxonMobil wasn't only selling gasoline. It was investing in
technology that aligned with the future of mobility.

### Media and Metrics
The communications campaign reached both mainstream and trade media. We
landed coverage in Wired, TechCrunch, Convenience Store News, and other
top publications. Within seven months, the campaigns generated 4.5
billion impressions with an advertising equivalent value of \$20
million.

Behind those numbers was constant testing. My team ran A/B tests on
content, analyzed web traffic, and adjusted SEO strategies. We learned
what headlines worked, which storylines performed best, and how digital
channels could reinforce the brand message.

### Lessons Learned
The Synergy fuels launch taught me that a product is only half the
story. The other half is how you frame it for the world. A campaign of
this scale requires alignment between research, marketing, design, and
digital engagement. It also requires the ability to translate technical
language into customer value.

For me, the project was a chance to bridge those worlds. From shaping
media coverage to adapting messages across countries, I saw firsthand
how communications can drive the success of a global launch.
